General Properties¶

As seen above, there are 21 columns with 10,866 rows (movie entries). For this research project, there are only 5 columns of interest: genres, vote average, release year, adjusted budget, and adjusted revenue so the rest of the columns need to be removed. In addition, the nan values in the genre column need to be replaced with empty strings inorder to match the non-values in the other columns. Given that a movie production isn't possible without a revenue or budget and more than half of the values in these columns are 0, the values need to be replaced with the mean of the column.

Given that the overall number of films have been increasing over the years, it is preferable to graph the film genre popularity as a percentage of the total genres for each year rather than number of genres.

Graphing all the genres in one plot makes the visualization much more convoluted and thereby much harder to draw conclusions. It is easier to look at the top 7 genres of the earliest and final year.

It is interesting to see that almost all the popular movies since the 1960's are dramas followed by comedies. While there has been spikes in action and adventure, documentaries have dramatically gained more popularity. However, it is important to note that a movie can be labeled with multiple genres.

Conclusions¶

Drama is the most popular genre followed by comedy with documentary gaining much more popularity in recent years.

There may be a correlation between a movies budget and revenue given that the highest-grossing films tend to have the highest production costs while the other films have a much smaller budget.

Rating and movie revenue may also be correlated given that the top-grossing films tend to have a much higher vote average in contrast with other films.

While adventure and action the most popular genres among the top-grossing films, drama and comedy are the most popular among the other films.

Inferential statistics or machine learning were not used to complete this project, and thereby the findings are tentative.

Limitations¶

Missing data in the input data is a limitation given that about 10% of the data have null values. Moreover, more than half of the values in the budget/revenue and adjusted columns are 0. This may skew the analysis leading to biased or inaccurate analysis. The sample must be good enough to represent the population so that the analysis will have enough data to generalize the entire population.
